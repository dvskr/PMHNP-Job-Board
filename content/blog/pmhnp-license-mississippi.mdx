## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

RN endorsement typically takes **3-4 weeks**.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited advanced practice program. To prescribe controlled substances, complete a **Board-approved educational program** prior to application.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

Maintain active national certification.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your MS RN License

Mississippi is a **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) state**. If your primary state of residence is another NLC state, your multistate RN license is valid here.

### Step 4: Apply for MS APRN License

Submit your APRN application to the Mississippi Board of Nursing.

### Step 5: Establish a Collaborative Agreement

Establish a **career-long regulated collaborative agreement** with a physician or dentist.

- Prescribing protocol must be **filed with and approved** by the Board of Nursing
- Not active until Board approval is received

### Step 6: Register with the DEA

Apply for your federal DEA registration using your Mississippi practice address.

- **Fees:** ~$888 for 3 years

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## MS Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Mississippi operates under a **Reduced Practice** model.

**Collaborative agreement requirements:**
APRNs must operate under a collaborative/consultative relationship with a physician or dentist within an established protocol. This protocol must be filed with and approved by the Board of Nursing.

**Pending FPA legislation:**
The Mississippi legislature has considered legislation allowing NPs to end their collaborative agreements and practice independently after **3,600 supervised practice hours**.

**Day-to-day impact:**
Mississippi legally recognizes NPs as **primary care providers**. However, PMHNPs are **not** authorized to sign:
- ❌ DNR orders
- ❌ POST forms

## Prescriptive Authority in MS

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
PMHNPs can administer, dispense, prescribe, and procure medications including controlled substances with an approved collaborative protocol.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
PMHNPs may prescribe **Schedule II through V** controlled substances.

**Key requirements:**

| Requirement | Details |
|:---|:---|
| Board-approved education | Must complete before applying for CS authority |
| Collaborative protocol | Must be filed with and approved by the Board |
| DEA registration | Required for controlled substances |

## Telehealth Rules in MS

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Mississippi reimburses for live video telehealth and recognizes the patient's home as an eligible originating site. Supported by the South Central Telehealth Resource Center.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Mississippi is an NLC member. However, the **APRN Compact is not enacted**. Out-of-state PMHNPs must hold an active **Mississippi APRN license** and maintain a local collaborative agreement.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to MS

1. **Complete Your Controlled Substance CE:** Mississippi requires **5 CE hours in controlled substances** every renewal cycle — even if you don't actively prescribe them. Must be completed within the State Certification Period (Jan 1 odd years to Dec 31 even years).

2. **File Your Protocol for Board Approval:** Your collaborative agreement is **not active** until the Board of Nursing officially approves it. Don't assume it's effective upon signing.

3. **Prepare for CE Audits:** The Mississippi Board frequently audits CE compliance. Keep certificates for all **40 contact hours** safely filed.

4. **Monitor FPA Legislation:** Legislation has been introduced to allow experienced NPs to drop collaborative agreements after **3,600 hours** — stay engaged with state nursing associations.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Mississippi a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: No. Mississippi is a reduced practice state requiring a career-long collaborative agreement. FPA legislation (independence after 3,600 hours) has been introduced.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Mississippi prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes, with a Board-approved educational program, an active collaborative agreement, and DEA registration.

**Q: Is Mississippi part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Yes, for RNs. The APRN Compact is not enacted — a Mississippi-specific APRN license is required.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my MS PMHNP license?**
A: Complete 40 CE hours every two years, including at least 5 in controlled substances. Maintain national certification.
