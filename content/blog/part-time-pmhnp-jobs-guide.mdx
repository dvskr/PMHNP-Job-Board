Not every PMHNP wants — or needs — a 40-hour work week. Whether you're supplementing a primary role, easing into retirement, managing family responsibilities, or simply prioritizing work-life balance, part-time psychiatric NP work has never been more accessible.

## Types of Part-Time PMHNP Positions

### Per Diem / PRN Shifts
- **Hours:** As-needed, typically 8-12 hour shifts
- **Pay:** $65-$120/hour (premium rates for no guaranteed hours)
- **Settings:** Hospitals, crisis centers, community mental health
- **Benefits:** Maximum flexibility, highest hourly rates
- **Drawbacks:** No guaranteed hours or benefits, unpredictable scheduling

### Half-Time W2 Positions
- **Hours:** 20-24 hours/week, set schedule
- **Pay:** $55,000-$85,000/year (pro-rated from full-time)
- **Settings:** Group practices, outpatient clinics, VA
- **Benefits:** Often includes pro-rated health insurance, retirement
- **Drawbacks:** Lower total income, may still require minimum patient volume

### Weekend/Evening-Only Roles
- **Hours:** 16-24 hours over weekends or evening blocks
- **Pay:** $70-$110/hour (shift differential premiums)
- **Settings:** Inpatient psychiatry, crisis stabilization, ERs
- **Benefits:** Premium pay, weekdays free
- **Drawbacks:** Disruptive schedule, higher acuity patients

### 1099 Contract / Telehealth Side Gig
- **Hours:** You choose — typically 5-20 hours/week
- **Pay:** $75-$150/hour
- **Settings:** Telehealth platforms, private practice overflow
- **Benefits:** Total schedule control, location independence
- **Drawbacks:** Self-employment tax, no benefits, admin burden

For a detailed comparison of 1099 vs W2 models, see our [1099 vs W2 Guide](/resources/1099-vs-w2).

## Building a Part-Time Portfolio Career

Many PMHNPs create a "portfolio" of part-time roles that collectively provide full-time income with maximum variety:

**Example portfolio:**
- **Telehealth platform (1099):** 12 patients/week, Tuesday-Thursday mornings → $72,000/year
- **Hospital per diem:** 2 weekend shifts/month → $28,000/year
- **Private practice (own):** 6 patients/week, Monday afternoons → $48,000/year
- **Total:** ~$148,000/year with extreme flexibility

This approach also provides professional resilience — if one income source disappears, you have others.

## Maintaining Benefits on Part-Time Income

The biggest concern with part-time work is losing employer benefits. Solutions:

| Benefit | Part-Time Solution |
|---------|-------------------|
| Health insurance | Spouse's plan, ACA marketplace ($400-$1,200/month), or VA part-time (20+ hrs gets benefits) |
| Retirement | SEP-IRA or Solo 401(k) on 1099 income (up to $66K/year) |
| Malpractice | Individual policy ($1,500-$3,000/year) or employer-provided on W2 shifts |
| CME/education | Self-funded ($1,000-$3,000/year) — tax-deductible on 1099 income |
| PTO | Built into your flexible schedule — you choose when not to work |

## Setting Boundaries as a Part-Time Provider

The biggest risk of part-time work is scope creep. Set these boundaries early:

- **In writing:** Get your schedule, patient volume expectations, and on-call requirements documented in your contract
- **After-hours coverage:** Clarify who handles patient emergencies on your off days
- **Gradual ramp expectations:** Don't let "part-time" quietly become 35+ hours
- **Documentation time:** Ensure patient hours don't consume all your scheduled time (budget 10-15 minutes per patient for notes)

## The Bottom Line

Part-time PMHNP work is a legitimate and increasingly popular career model. With hourly rates of $60-$120+, the math often works out better per hour than full-time positions — especially in 1099 or per diem arrangements.
