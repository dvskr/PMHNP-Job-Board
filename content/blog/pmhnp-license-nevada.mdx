## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

Nevada is **not** an NLC state — all out-of-state nurses must apply by endorsement. Processing ranges from **2-16 weeks**.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited advanced practice program. For prescriptive authority, complete at least **2 semester credits in advanced pharmacotherapeutics** as part of your program or within 2 years before application.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

Maintain active national certification.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your NV RN License

Nevada has pending NLC legislation (SB 34) but currently requires a **single-state Nevada RN license**.

### Step 4: Apply for Your NV APRN License

Submit your APRN application to the Nevada State Board of Nursing.

### Step 5: Obtain Prescriptive Authority & DEA Registration

Apply for prescriptive authority through the Board — grants independent prescribing for Schedule III-V. Then apply for your federal DEA registration.

- **DEA fees:** ~$888 for 3 years
- No separate state controlled substance certificate required

### Step 6: Complete Schedule II Transition (If Applicable)

| Experience Level | Schedule II Access |
|:---|:---|
| **< 2 years or < 2,000 hours** | Must prescribe under physician-approved protocol |
| **2+ years or 2,000+ hours** | Full independent Schedule II prescribing |

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## NV Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Nevada is a **Full Practice Authority** state.

**No collaborative agreement needed:**
APRNs independently evaluate, diagnose, and prescribe without a career-long collaborative agreement. The Board officially recognizes mental health as a designated population of focus.

**Day-to-day impact:**
PMHNPs in Nevada are recognized as **primary care providers** with significant clinical autonomy:
- ✅ Sign POLST forms
- ✅ Sign medical certifications
- ✅ Petition for emergency/involuntary admission to mental health facilities

> **Schedule II exception:** New graduates must practice **2 years or 2,000 hours** under physician protocol before independently prescribing Schedule II drugs.

## Prescriptive Authority in NV

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
APRNs with prescriptive authority can prescribe, administer, dispense, and procure dangerous drugs, poisons, and devices.

**Schedule II-V rules:**

| Schedule | Access |
|:---|:---|
| **Schedule III-V** | Independent prescribing immediately |
| **Schedule II** | Independent after 2 years / 2,000 hours (physician protocol required before) |

**No separate state CS certificate** — just DEA registration required.

## Telehealth Rules in NV

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Nevada has established telehealth parity laws and recognizes the patient's home as an eligible originating site.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Nevada has **not** enacted the NLC or APRN Compact. Out-of-state PMHNPs must hold an active **single-state Nevada APRN license** to treat patients in NV.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to NV

1. **Plan for Endorsement Delays:** Nevada is not a compact state. Board processing can take **2-16 weeks** — apply well before your target start date.

2. **Track Your Schedule II Hours:** Document your first **2,000 hours** (or 2 years) under physician protocol to transition to fully independent Schedule II prescribing.

3. **Complete the Bioterrorism CE:** Nevada requires a **one-time 4-hour CE course** on bioterrorism — unique to this state.

4. **Understand Your CE Load:** Biennial renewal requires **45 CE hours** including 15 in your specialty, 2 in substance abuse, and 4 in suicide prevention (every 4 years).

5. **Consider Rural Nevada:** Elko, Nye, and other rural counties have extreme shortages with maximum NHSC eligibility and often premium compensation packages.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Nevada a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: Yes. APRNs can independently evaluate, diagnose, and prescribe without a career-long collaborative agreement.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Nevada prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes, but with a transition period. APRNs need 2 years or 2,000 hours of clinical experience before independently prescribing Schedule II drugs. Before that, a physician-approved protocol is required.

**Q: Is Nevada part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Not yet. Legislation (SB 34) is pending. Out-of-state nurses must apply for a single-state Nevada license.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my NV PMHNP license?**
A: Complete 45 CE hours every two years, including 15 in your specialty and 2 in substance abuse. Additionally, 4 hours in suicide prevention every 4 years and a one-time 4-hour bioterrorism course.
