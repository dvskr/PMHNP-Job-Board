## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

Alaska is **not** a compact state. Endorsement takes **8-16 weeks** — plan well ahead.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited advanced practice program. Must include **15 hours** of advanced pharmacology and clinical management of drug therapy.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

Maintain active national certification.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your AK RN License

Alaska is **not** an NLC state (HB 131/SB 124 pending). You must apply for a **single-state Alaska RN license** by endorsement.

### Step 4: Apply for Your AK APRN License

Submit your APRN application to the Alaska Board of Nursing.

### Step 5: Obtain Prescriptive Authority

Prescriptive authority for Schedule II-V is **included automatically** in your APRN licensure — no separate application needed. Just meet the 15-hour pharmacology requirement.

### Step 6: Register with the DEA

Apply for your federal DEA registration using your Alaska practice address.

- **Fees:** ~$888 for 3 years

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## AK Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Alaska is a **Full Practice Authority** state — no transition, no collaborative agreement.

APRNs are **licensed independent practitioners** who can evaluate patients, diagnose conditions, and prescribe medications entirely without physician oversight. Alaska formally recognizes NPs as **primary care providers**.

**Day-to-day impact:**
You can independently:
- ✅ Sign POLST forms
- ✅ Declare death and sign death certificates
- ✅ Issue DNR orders

---

## Prescriptive Authority in AK

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
APRNs may independently prescribe prescription drugs, legend drugs, and controlled substances. Authority is **included directly in your licensure** — no separate application.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Full independent prescribing of **Schedule II through V** controlled substances.

**Requirements:**
- 15 hours of advanced pharmacology
- Federal DEA registration
- No physician involvement or collaborative agreement

## Telehealth Rules in AK

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — telehealth is essential for delivering care across Alaska's vast frontier regions. Medicaid reimburses live video telehealth services.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Alaska has **not** enacted NLC or APRN Compact. Out-of-state PMHNPs must hold an active **single-state Alaska APRN license**.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to AK

1. **Plan for Long Endorsement Timelines:** Alaska takes **8-16 weeks** for endorsement processing — begin paperwork months before your start date.

2. **Track Your Prescribing CEs:** Renewal requires 30 CE hours plus **15 pharmacology hours** for prescribers.

3. **Fulfill the Opioid Mandate:** DEA holders must complete **2 CE hours** in pain management, opioid use, and addiction every renewal period.

4. **Advocate for the Compact:** Pending legislation (HB 131, SB 124) to join the NLC is supported by 89% of resident nurses. Stay engaged with the Alaska Nurses Association.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Alaska a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: Yes. PMHNPs are licensed independent practitioners with no physician oversight or collaborative agreements.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Alaska prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes. Prescriptive authority is included in your licensure — no separate application. Just register with the DEA.

**Q: Is Alaska part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Not yet. Legislation (HB 131, SB 124) is pending. A single-state Alaska license is currently required.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my AK PMHNP license?**
A: Complete 30 CE hours every two years plus 15 pharmacology hours. DEA holders need 2 additional hours in opioid/pain management.
