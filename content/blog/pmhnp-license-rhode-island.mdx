## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

Rhode Island recently joined the NLC. Single-state endorsement takes **7-10 weeks**.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited advanced practice program with a psychiatric-mental health concentration.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

Maintain active national certification.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your RI RN License

Rhode Island is a **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) state**. If your primary state of residence is another NLC state, your multistate RN license covers you here.

### Step 4: Apply for Your RI APRN License

Submit your **Certified Registered Nurse Practitioner (CRNP)** application to the Rhode Island Board of Nursing.

### Step 5: Obtain Prescriptive Authority

Prescriptive authority is **granted alongside your advanced practice licensure** — no separate application.

### Step 6: Register with the DEA

Apply for your federal DEA registration using your Rhode Island practice address.

- **Fees:** ~$888 for 3 years

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## RI Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Rhode Island is a **Full Practice Authority** state — no transition, no collaborative agreement.

APRNs can independently evaluate, diagnose, prescribe, and provide therapeutic measures. NPs are **legally recognized as primary care providers** by state statute.

**Day-to-day impact:**
You can independently sign:
- ✅ Death certificates
- ✅ DNR orders
- ✅ MOLST forms

## Prescriptive Authority in RI

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
CRNPs have full authority to prescribe, administer, dispense, and procure legend medications independently.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Full independent prescribing of **Schedule II through V** controlled substances.

> **Psych-specific note:** Rhode Island law **explicitly authorizes** psychiatric/mental health NPs to prescribe Schedule II stimulants — critical for ADHD treatment.

**Cannot prescribe:** Schedule I controlled substances.

## Telehealth Rules in RI

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Rhode Island offers broad live video reimbursement and recognizes the patient's home as an eligible originating site.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
RI is an NLC member. The **APRN Compact is not enacted** — out-of-state PMHNPs must hold an active **RI APRN license**.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to RI

1. **Plan for Endorsement Timelines:** Single-state endorsement takes **7-10 weeks** — begin paperwork early.

2. **Track Your Practice Hours:** Biennial renewal requires documentation of **400 practice hours** at the APRN level.

3. **Audit Your CE Requirements:** General renewal needs 10 CE hrs (incl. 2 in substance abuse). Prescribers also need **30 pharmacology hours every 6 years**.

4. **Embrace Your Autonomy:** You're a legally defined PCP — sign MOLST, DNR, and death certificates independently.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Rhode Island a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: Yes, with no transition period. APRNs are legally recognized as primary care providers.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Rhode Island prescribe Schedule II controlled substances like ADHD stimulants?**
A: Yes. RI regulations explicitly authorize psychiatric/mental health NPs to prescribe Schedule II stimulants.

**Q: Is Rhode Island part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Yes. The APRN Compact is not enacted — an RI APRN license is required.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my RI PMHNP license?**
A: Complete 10 CE hours every two years (incl. 2 in substance abuse) plus 400 practice hours. Prescribers need 30 pharmacology hours every 6 years.
