You've heard PMHNPs can earn $200K-$300K+ in private practice. But what do the real numbers look like? In this deep dive, we'll break down actual revenue models, itemize every overhead expense, and show you what owners actually take home across different practice models.

## Revenue Models: How Private Practice PMHNPs Generate Income

### Model 1: Insurance-Based Practice

| Revenue Component | Per Visit | Annual (24 pts/week, 48 weeks) |
|-------------------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Initial evaluations (90792) | $195-$250 | $46,800-$60,000 (240 evals) |
| Med management follow-ups (99213-99215) | $95-$165 | $102,600-$178,200 (1,080 visits) |
| Psychotherapy add-on (90833) | $50-$75 | $18,000-$27,000 (360 sessions) |
| **Total gross revenue** | | **$167,400-$265,200** |

*Revenue will be higher if you see 28+ patients/week or add specialty services.*

### Model 2: Cash-Pay / Self-Pay Practice

| Revenue Component | Per Visit | Annual (22 pts/week, 48 weeks) |
|-------------------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Initial evaluations | $275-$400 | $66,000-$96,000 (240 evals) |
| Follow-up visits | $150-$250 | $113,400-$189,000 (756 visits) |
| Extended sessions (therapy component) | $200-$350 | $38,400-$67,200 (192 sessions) |
| **Total gross revenue** | | **$217,800-$352,200** |

### Model 3: Hybrid (Insurance + Cash-Pay)

Most successful practices operate a hybrid model:
- Accept 3-4 major insurance panels for patient volume
- Offer cash-pay for patients without coverage or those preferring privacy
- Charge full cash rate for no-shows and late cancellations
- Typical mix: 60% insurance / 40% cash-pay

## Tax Strategies for Practice Owners

As a practice owner, you have access to powerful tax optimization strategies:

1. **S-Corp election** — When net income exceeds ~$80K, saves on self-employment tax
2. **SEP-IRA / Solo 401(k)** — Contribute up to $66,000+ per year
3. **Home office deduction** — $1,500 simplified or actual expenses
4. **Vehicle / mileage** — If seeing patients in multiple locations
5. **QBI deduction** — 20% qualified business income deduction (consult your CPA)
