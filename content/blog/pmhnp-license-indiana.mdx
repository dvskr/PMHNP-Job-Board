## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

The Indiana Professional Licensing Agency (PLA) manages nursing applications via the **MyLicense One** portal.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited master's or doctoral program. Transcripts must show successful completion of a graduate-level pharmacology course.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

National certification is required to validate your specialty training.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your IN RN License

Indiana is a **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) state**. If your primary state of residence is another NLC state, your multistate RN license is valid here.

- If applying by endorsement: **$50**, processing **4-6 weeks**
- Requires criminal background check + Nursys license verification

### Step 4: Apply for Your IN APRN License

Submit the APRN Prescriptive Authority application via the **MyLicense One** portal.

| Item | Cost |
|:---|:---|
| APRN Application Fee | $50 |
| Documents required | Official transcripts + proof of active RN license |

### Step 5: Establish a Collaborative Agreement with a Physician

Establish a **written collaborative practice agreement** with an Indiana-licensed practitioner.

- Agreement must detail how you cooperate, coordinate, and consult on patient care
- Must specify geographic proximity and emergency coverage
- **Includes 5% chart review requirement** (see Practice Authority section)

### Step 6: Obtain CSR and DEA Registration

1. First obtain your **Indiana Controlled Substance Registration (CSR)** — **$60 fee**
2. Then apply for your **federal DEA registration** (~$888 for 3 years)

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## IN Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Indiana is a **Reduced Practice** state.

**Collaborative agreement requirements:**
To practice as an APRN with prescriptive authority, maintain a written collaborative practice agreement with a licensed practitioner. The agreement must detail how you share practice trends, maintain geographic proximity, and provide emergency coverage.

**5% chart review mandate:**
Your collaborating physician must conduct timely review of your prescribing practices, including at least a **5% random sampling** of your patient charts and prescribed medications.

**Pending FPA legislation:**
Legislation has been recently introduced to remove the requirement for APRNs to maintain a collaborative agreement with a supervising physician.

> **Day-to-day impact:** While you can evaluate and diagnose patients, your prescribing practices are subject to physician review. Build the 5% chart review quota directly into your clinic's administrative workflow.

## Prescriptive Authority in IN

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
Under a collaborative practice agreement, PMHNPs can administer, dispense, and prescribe legend drugs and devices.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Indiana PMHNPs can prescribe **Schedule II through V** controlled substances.

**Key restriction:**

| Rule | Details |
|:---|:---|
| Weight reduction prohibition | APRNs **cannot** prescribe Schedule II controlled substances for weight reduction or obesity control |
| CSR + DEA required | Must hold both state CSR and federal DEA |

## Telehealth Rules in IN

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Indiana reimburses for live video telehealth services and recognizes the patient's home as an eligible originating site.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Indiana is a full NLC member. However, the **APRN Compact is not enacted** — out-of-state PMHNPs must obtain an active Indiana APRN license **and** maintain an Indiana collaborative agreement.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities allowing providers to prescribe controlled substances via telemedicine have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to IN

1. **Prepare for Multiple Fees:** Budget for RN endorsement ($50) + APRN application ($50) + CSR ($60) = **$160 total** before DEA costs.

2. **Track Your Chart Reviews:** Your collaborative agreement legally mandates **5% random chart sampling** by your collaborating physician. Build this into your clinic's administrative workflow.

3. **Audit Your Pharmacology CEs:** To renew prescriptive authority, complete **30 CE hours** every two years with at least **8 in pharmacology** and **2 in opioid prescribing/abuse**.

4. **Update Your CSR Location:** Your CSR is tied to your practice location. If you change jobs, update your practice location via the **MyLicense One** portal to match your collaborative agreement.

5. **Consider Rural Indiana:** Southern and eastern Indiana have among the state's most severe provider shortages, offering maximum NHSC loan repayment eligibility and often signing bonuses.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Indiana a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: No. Indiana is a reduced practice state. APRNs must maintain a written collaborative practice agreement with an Indiana-licensed practitioner. Legislation to remove this requirement has been introduced.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Indiana prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes, with a collaborative agreement, Indiana CSR, and federal DEA number. However, APRNs cannot prescribe Schedule II drugs for weight reduction or obesity control.

**Q: Is Indiana part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Yes. Indiana is an active NLC member, allowing RNs with a multistate license to practice in the state. The APRN Compact has not been enacted.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my IN PMHNP license?**
A: Renew every two years (odd-numbered years). Complete 30 CE hours including at least 8 in pharmacology and 2 in opioid prescribing and abuse.
