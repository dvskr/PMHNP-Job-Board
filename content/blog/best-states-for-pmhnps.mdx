Where you choose to practice doesn't just determine your paycheck — it defines the scope of your clinical authority, your take-home pay after taxes, your housing costs, and your overall quality of life. A PMHNP earning $165,000 in Washington State (FPA + no state income tax) takes home significantly more than a PMHNP earning $185,000 in California (restricted practice + 13% state tax + high cost of living).

This guide ranks states not just by salary, but by the factors that actually determine your financial and professional outcome.

## Understanding Practice Authority: Why It Matters

Your ability to practice independently, prescribe without co-signatures, and open your own practice depends entirely on state law.

### Full Practice Authority (FPA) States

*You practice independently — no physician oversight required.*

These states allow PMHNPs to assess, diagnose, prescribe (including controlled substances), and manage patients independently from day one (or after a brief transition period):

WA, OR, NV, AZ, NM, CO, WY, MT, ID, ND, SD, MN, IA, NE, KS, UT, ME, NH, VT, MA, RI, CT, NY (after 3,600 hours), MD, DE, VA (after 5 years), DC, HI, AK

### Reduced Practice Authority States

*You need a collaboration agreement but can practice with general supervision.*

CA, TX, IL, IN, WI, NJ, PA (varies by specialty)

### Restricted Practice Authority States

*You need direct physician oversight and/or co-signing of prescriptions.*

FL, GA, NC, SC, TN, AL, MS, MO, OK, MI, OH

**Why FPA matters financially:** In FPA states, you can open a private practice without paying a collaborating physician ($500-$2,000/month in restricted states). This directly increases your income. FPA states also have faster credentialing, fewer administrative hurdles, and more practice options.

For your state's specific practice requirements, see our [Full Practice Authority Guide](/resources/fpa-guide) and [50-state licensing guides](/jobs/locations).

## Where Should You Move? A Decision Framework

### For Maximum Take-Home Pay:
→ **Washington State** or **Nevada** (FPA + no income tax + strong salaries)

### For Private Practice Independence:
→ **Arizona** or **Colorado** (FPA + growing markets + low barriers to entry)

### For Maximum Raw Salary:
→ **California** or **New York** (but prepare for high taxes and COL)

### For Low Cost of Living + High Relative Value:
→ **Iowa, Nebraska, or Kansas** (FPA + very low COL + competitive salaries relative to expenses)

### For VA/Federal Employment:
→ **Any state** — VA provides FPA nationwide regardless of state law, with consistent federal pay scales

### For New Grads Seeking Mentorship:
→ **States with large health systems** — Massachusetts, New York, California, Texas have the most residency and fellowship programs
