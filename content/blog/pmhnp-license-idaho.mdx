## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

Idaho is an NLC state. Single-state endorsement takes approximately **8 weeks**.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited advanced practice program. Must include **30 hours of post-basic pharmacotherapeutics** education.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

Maintain active national certification.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your ID RN License

Idaho is a **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) state**. If your primary state of residence is another NLC state, your multistate RN license covers you here.

### Step 4: Apply for Your ID APRN License

Submit your APRN application to the Idaho Board of Nursing.

### Step 5: Obtain Prescriptive Authority

Idaho grants **full prescriptive authority** for Schedule II-V upon licensure, provided the pharmacotherapeutics education requirement is met. No collaborative agreement needed.

### Step 6: Register with the DEA

Apply for your federal DEA registration using your Idaho practice address.

- **Fees:** ~$888 for 3 years

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## ID Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Idaho is a **Full Practice Authority** state — no transition, no collaborative agreement.

APRNs assume **primary responsibility** for patient care. You can independently:
- Evaluate patients and formulate diagnoses
- Manage acute and chronic health conditions
- Prescribe medications and therapeutic measures

**Day-to-day impact:**
Idaho **legally recognizes NPs as primary care providers**. You can independently sign:
- ✅ POST forms
- ✅ DNR orders
- ✅ Workers' compensation forms
- ✅ Death certificates

## Prescriptive Authority in ID

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
APRNs are licensed independent practitioners authorized to independently prescribe, administer, and dispense therapeutic pharmacologic agents.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Full independent prescribing of **Schedule II through V** controlled substances — no restrictions, no transition period.

**Requirements:**
- 30 hours of post-basic pharmacotherapeutics education
- Active federal DEA registration

## Telehealth Rules in ID

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Idaho provides Medicaid reimbursement for live video telehealth and RPM. Supported by the Northwest Regional Telehealth Resource Center.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Idaho is an NLC member. The **APRN Compact is not enacted** — out-of-state PMHNPs must hold an active **Idaho APRN license**.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to ID

1. **Plan for Endorsement Timeframes:** Single-state RN endorsement can take **~8 weeks** — begin paperwork well before your start date.

2. **Leverage Your PCP Status:** Utilize your legal recognition as a primary care provider to sign POST forms, DNR orders, and workers' comp forms independently.
