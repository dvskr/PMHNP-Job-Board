## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

New Mexico is an NLC state. Single-state endorsement typically takes **4+ weeks**.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited advanced practice program. Must complete **24 contact hours of pharmacology** coursework.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

Maintain active national certification.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your NM RN License

New Mexico is a **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) state**. If your primary state of residence is another NLC state, your multistate RN license is valid here.

### Step 4: Apply for Your NM CNP License

Submit your **Certified Nurse Practitioner** application to the New Mexico Board of Nursing.

### Step 5: Complete 400 Hours for Independent Prescribing

To independently prescribe dangerous drugs, verify **400 hours** of:
- Work experience prescribing dangerous drugs, **or**
- A prescribing preceptorship

### Step 6: Register with Board of Pharmacy & DEA

| Credential | Details |
|:---|:---|
| **NM Board of Pharmacy** | Required state controlled substance license |
| **Federal DEA registration** | ~$888 for 3 years |

> **Important:** You need **both** — Board of Pharmacy registration is separate from your DEA number.

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## NM Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** New Mexico is a **Full Practice Authority** pioneer — one of the earliest FPA states in the nation.

**No collaborative agreement needed:**
CNPs can independently make decisions regarding patient needs, carry out health regimens, and serve as primary acute, chronic, and end-of-life healthcare providers. The only supervised requirement is the **400-hour prescribing preceptorship** for independent prescriptive authority.

**Day-to-day impact:**
New Mexico **legally recognizes NPs as primary care providers** and requires insurers to cover NP services. You can sign:
- ✅ MOST/POLST forms
- ✅ Advanced directives
- ✅ DNR orders
- ✅ Death certificates

## Prescriptive Authority in NM

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
CNPs with 400-hour experience can independently prescribe and distribute dangerous drugs and controlled substances.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Full independent prescribing of **Schedule II through V** controlled substances.

**Requirements:**
- 400-hour prescribing experience/preceptorship
- NM Board of Pharmacy registration
- Federal DEA registration

## Telehealth Rules in NM

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — New Mexico offers Medicaid reimbursement for live video telehealth and RPM.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
New Mexico is an NLC member. However, the **APRN Compact is not enacted** — out-of-state PMHNPs must obtain an active **New Mexico CNP license**.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to NM

1. **Plan for the 400-Hour Rule:** Secure a preceptorship or log 400 hours of prescribing experience before gaining independent prescriptive authority.

2. **Audit Your CEs Based on DEA Status:**
   - **Without DEA:** 10 hrs pharmacology + 10 hrs specialty
   - **With DEA:** 10 hrs pharmacology + 5 hrs specialty + **5 hrs non-cancer pain management**

3. **Register with the Board of Pharmacy:** Controlled substance prescribing requires NM Board of Pharmacy registration **in addition to** your federal DEA number.

4. **Leverage the Cost of Living:** NM's #1 COL-adjusted salary ranking means your purchasing power is unmatched — use this in contract negotiations.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is New Mexico a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: Yes. New Mexico is an FPA pioneer. CNPs are legally recognized as primary care providers with full clinical independence.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in New Mexico prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes, after completing a 400-hour prescribing preceptorship/experience and registering with both the NM Board of Pharmacy and DEA.

**Q: Is New Mexico part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Yes. The APRN Compact is not enacted — a New Mexico CNP license is required.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my NM PMHNP license?**
A: Complete 30 CE hours every two years. DEA-licensed CNPs must include 10 hrs pharmacology, 5 hrs specialty, and 5 hrs non-cancer pain management.
