California and Texas together represent nearly 20% of all PMHNP job openings nationwide. Whether you're a new grad deciding where to start your career or an experienced psychiatric nurse practitioner considering relocation, these two state markets deserve a deep dive.

## California PMHNP Market Overview

### Job Market Snapshot

- **Total PMHNP openings:** 2,500+ (highest in the US)
- **Average salary:** $181,670 (5th highest nationally)
- **Practice authority:** Restricted → Transitioning under AB 890
- **Top metro areas:** Los Angeles, San Francisco, San Diego, Sacramento
- **Mental health shortage:** 46 of 58 counties are designated MHPSAs

### Practice Authority: AB 890 Changes Everything

California has historically been a restricted practice state, requiring physician supervision for PMHNPs. However, **Assembly Bill 890** is changing the landscape:

- **Phase 1 (current):** NPs with 3+ years of experience in specified settings can apply for independent practice authority
- **Phase 2 (expected 2026-2027):** Broader independent practice for qualified NPs
- **Impact:** This transition makes California increasingly attractive for PMHNPs — expect FPA-equivalent practice within 2-3 years

See our [Full Practice Authority Guide](/resources/fpa-guide) for the complete state breakdown.

### California Salary Breakdown

| Setting | Salary Range |
|---------|-------------|
| Outpatient/Community Health | $155,000-$180,000 |
| Hospital/Inpatient | $165,000-$195,000 |
| Private Practice | $180,000-$250,000+ |
| Telehealth/Remote | $150,000-$200,000 |
| VA/Federal | $140,000-$175,000 + federal benefits |

### Top Employers in California

1. **Kaiser Permanente** — Largest integrated health system, excellent benefits, union-represented
2. **Sutter Health** — Major Northern California system with psychiatric programs
3. **UCLA Health** — Academic medical center with research opportunities
4. **LA County DMH** — Government positions with loan forgiveness
5. **Telehealth platforms** — Talkiatry, Cerebral, Lyra (based in the Bay Area)

### California Licensing Requirements

- **Supervisory agreement** required (until AB 890 fully implements)
- **Board of Registered Nursing (BRN)** processes APRN applications
- **Processing time:** 8-16 weeks (one of the slower states)
- **DEA registration** required for prescribing controlled substances
- **Continuing education:** 30 hours every 2 years

## Texas PMHNP Market Overview

### Job Market Snapshot

- **Total PMHNP openings:** 2,240+ (3rd highest nationally)
- **Average salary:** $150,000-$175,000
- **Practice authority:** Restricted (physician supervision required)
- **Top metro areas:** Houston, Dallas-Fort Worth, San Antonio, Austin
- **Mental health shortage:** 207 of 254 counties are designated MHPSAs

### Practice Authority: Still Restricted

Texas remains a restricted practice state with no imminent legislative changes:

- **Prescriptive authority agreement** required with a supervising physician
- **Physician delegation** must be within a 75-mile radius (with exceptions)
- **Controlled substances:** Schedule III-V with physician agreement; Schedule II requires additional authorization
- **Impact on practice:** Finding a collaborative physician adds cost ($500-$2,000/month) but is manageable

### Texas Salary Breakdown

| Setting | Salary Range |
|---------|-------------|
| Outpatient/Community Health | $135,000-$160,000 |
| Hospital/Inpatient | $150,000-$180,000 |
| Private Practice | $160,000-$220,000+ |
| Telehealth/Remote | $130,000-$175,000 |
| Correctional/Federal | $140,000-$175,000 |

### Top Employers in Texas

1. **HCA Healthcare** — Largest for-profit hospital chain in the US, major TX presence
2. **Baylor Scott & White** — Largest non-profit health system in Texas
3. **Texas Health Resources** — Major DFW metro system
4. **UT Health** — Academic systems across the state
5. **Texas DSHS/HHSC** — State government positions with benefits and loan forgiveness

### Texas Licensing Requirements

- **Texas Board of Nursing (BON)** for APRN licensure
- **Prescriptive authority application** filed separately
- **Processing time:** 4-8 weeks (faster than California)
- **Supervisory agreement** must be on file with the BON
- **Continuing education:** 20 hours every 2 years (pharmacology required)

## Head-to-Head: California vs Texas

| Factor | 🏖️ California | 🤠 Texas |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Average salary** | $181,670 | $155,000 |
| **Cost of living** | Very High (1.4x national average) | Low-Moderate (0.9x) |
| **COL-adjusted salary** | ~$130,000 | ~$172,000 |
| **Practice authority** | Restricted → Transitioning | Restricted |
| **State income tax** | 9.3-13.3% | **0% (no state tax)** |
| **Job openings** | 2,500+ | 2,240+ |
| **Licensing speed** | 8-16 weeks | 4-8 weeks |
| **Shortage severity** | 46/58 counties | 207/254 counties |

### The Bottom Line

- **For maximum take-home pay:** Texas wins when you factor in zero state income tax and lower cost of living. A $155K salary in Texas has more purchasing power than $181K in California.
- **For future practice autonomy:** California is moving toward FPA under AB 890, making it increasingly attractive for PMHNPs who want independent practice.
- **For new grads:** Texas's faster licensing, lower cost of living, and massive shortage areas make it a strong starting market.
- **For private practice:** Both states have attractive markets, but California's larger population and higher reimbursement rates can yield higher gross revenue.
