"Locum tenens" — Latin for "to hold the place" — has become one of the most lucrative and adventurous career paths for psychiatric nurse practitioners. If you've ever wanted to explore different states, work with diverse patient populations, and earn premium pay while doing it, locum tenens work might be your ideal career model.

## What is Locum Tenens PMHNP Work?

Locum tenens PMHNPs fill temporary staffing needs at healthcare facilities across the country. You're placed by a staffing agency at a facility that needs coverage due to:

- Provider vacancies or recruitment gaps
- Leave coverage (maternity, medical, sabbatical)
- Census spikes or seasonal demand
- Rural/underserved facilities struggling to recruit permanent staff
- New program launches needing experienced providers

### Typical Assignment Structure
- **Duration:** 2 weeks to 12 months (13 weeks is the most common)
- **Schedule:** Usually full-time (40 hrs/week), sometimes 4x10 or 3x12
- **Setting:** Inpatient psych units, outpatient clinics, community health centers, correctional facilities, VA hospitals
- **Location:** Nationwide — rural assignments pay the most

## How Much Do Locum Tenens PMHNPs Earn?

Locum tenens is consistently the highest-paying PMHNP employment model:

| Component | Typical Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| Hourly rate | $100-$175/hour |
| Weekly gross | $4,000-$7,000 |
| Annual (48 weeks) | $192,000-$336,000 |
| Housing stipend | $1,500-$3,500/month (or furnished housing provided) |
| Travel reimbursement | $500-$2,000 per assignment |
| Malpractice insurance | Included by agency |

### Tax Advantages of Locum Work

If you maintain a permanent "tax home" (your primary residence), portions of your locum income may qualify as tax-free:

- **Housing stipend:** Often tax-free if you maintain a tax home and duplicate housing
- **Per diem (meals & incidentals):** GSA per diem rates for your assignment location
- **Travel expenses:** Flights, rental car, or mileage to/from assignments
- **Important:** Consult a CPA experienced with travel healthcare — IRS rules around tax homes are specific

### Tips for Choosing an Agency

1. **Work with 2-3 agencies simultaneously** — you're not exclusive to one
2. **Ask about their reimbursement timeline** — weekly pay is standard; avoid agencies that pay monthly
3. **Verify malpractice coverage** — should be occurrence-based, not claims-made
4. **Check credentialing speed** — good agencies credential in 30-60 days; slow ones take 90+
5. **Read the cancellation clause** — understand what happens if the facility cancels your assignment

## How to Get Started in Locum Tenens

### Requirements
- Active APRN license (you'll need licensing in the assignment state)
- ANCC PMHNP-BC certification
- DEA registration
- 1-2+ years of clinical experience (preferred by most facilities)
- CAQH ProView profile
- Current BLS/ACLS certifications

### Getting Your First Assignment

1. **Contact 2-3 locum agencies** — share your CV, license states, availability, and preferences
2. **Your recruiter will match you** — they'll present assignments matching your criteria
3. **Review and negotiate** — rate, schedule, housing, and travel details
4. **Credentialing** — the agency handles facility credentialing (30-90 days)
5. **Start your assignment** — orientation, EMR training, then full practice

### What to Negotiate

- **Hourly rate:** Always negotiate — first offers are typically 10-20% below ceiling
- **Housing:** Furnished apartment > stipend if you're unfamiliar with the area
- **Extension bonus:** If extending past the initial term, negotiate a rate increase
- **Licensing costs:** Agency should reimburse licensing fees for assignment states
- **Cancellation protection:** 30-day minimum notice if facility cancels

## What to Expect on Your First Assignment

### The First Week
- Facility orientation and credentialing
- EMR training (every facility uses different systems)
- Meeting your team — nurses, social workers, techs
- Starting with a lighter patient load while you learn workflows

### Common Challenges
- **Adapting to new EMRs quickly** — document templates vary widely
- **Building rapport with staff fast** — you're the "outsider" initially
- **Variable practice patterns** — each facility has different protocols
- **Isolation** — especially in rural assignments; plan social activities

### What Locums Love About It
- **Adventure** — new cities, states, and patient populations every 3 months
- **Premium pay** — often earning 30-50% more than permanent colleagues
- **No office politics** — you're there to do a job, not play politics
- **Clinical variety** — different patient populations keep skills sharp
- **Freedom** — take a month off between assignments whenever you want

## The Bottom Line

Locum tenens PMHNP work is ideal for experienced clinicians who want maximum earning potential ($200K-$350K+), variety, and schedule freedom. While it requires adaptability and comfort with change, the financial and lifestyle rewards are unmatched.
