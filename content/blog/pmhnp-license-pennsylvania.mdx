## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

The licensure process in Pennsylvania is known for **lengthy processing times**, often taking **3+ months** for out-of-state endorsements. Start early and follow these steps:

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited program. To qualify for prescriptive authority in Pennsylvania, you must complete **45 hours of coursework in advanced pharmacology**.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

You must pass your national board certification exam and maintain active certification to practice.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your PA RN License

Pennsylvania is a member of the **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)**. If your primary state of residence is another compact state, your multistate RN license is valid here. Otherwise, apply for a single-state RN license.

### Step 4: Apply for Your PA CRNP License

Submit your Certified Registered Nurse Practitioner (CRNP) application via the state's **PALS portal**.

| Item | Cost |
|:---|:---|
| Initial Licensing Fee | $95 |
| FBI Background Check | Required (fingerprint-based) |

- **Processing time:** 3+ months for endorsement
- **Documents:** Transcripts, national certification, fingerprints

### Step 5: Establish a Collaborative Agreement

To practice legally, you must maintain a **career-long written agreement** with one or more physicians.

> **Critical:** If you want prescriptive authority, you must have a prescriptive authority collaborative agreement with **at least two physicians**. This is unique to Pennsylvania.

### Step 6: Register with the DEA

Apply for your federal DEA registration to prescribe controlled substances using your Pennsylvania practice address.

- **Fees:** ~$888 for 3 years

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## PA Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Pennsylvania is a **Reduced Practice** state.

**Collaborative agreement requirements:**
CRNPs must have a written collaboration agreement signed by both the CRNP and the collaborating physician(s). Under this agreement, a CRNP can perform comprehensive assessments, establish medical diagnoses, order and supervise diagnostic tests, and implement treatment plans.

**Act 112 & Pending Legislation:**
Advocacy efforts (such as State Bill 25) have pushed for Full Practice Authority after a 3-year/3,600-hour transition period. Check with the Board of Nursing regarding the current implementation status of Act 112.

> **Day-to-day impact:** Your clinical and prescriptive decisions are officially tied to the scope outlined in your written collaboration agreement, and your collaborating physician may review your patient records to ensure clinical competence.

## Prescriptive Authority in PA

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
When acting under a collaborative agreement, CRNPs can prescribe and dispense drugs, as well as order therapeutic or corrective measures.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Pennsylvania allows prescribing of **Schedule II through V** controlled substances with strict duration limits:

| Schedule | Maximum Supply |
|:---|:---|
| **Schedule II** | **30-day supply** |
| **Schedule III-V** | **90-day supply** |

**Key requirement:**
To maintain prescriptive authority, you must maintain agreements with **at least two physicians**.

## Telehealth Rules in PA

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Pennsylvania features Medicaid reimbursement for live video and remote patient monitoring (RPM). PA also permits initial opioid treatment program exams for medications like buprenorphine to be conducted via telehealth, provided an in-person follow-up occurs within 14 days.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Pennsylvania has joined the NLC, making cross-border practice easier for RNs. However, because the **APRN Compact is not enacted**, out-of-state PMHNPs must still hold a **Pennsylvania CRNP license** to treat patients located in PA.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA flexibilities allowing the prescribing of controlled substances via telemedicine remain subject to ongoing federal extensions through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to PA

1. **Plan for Long Licensing Delays:** The Pennsylvania Board of Nursing can take **3+ months** to process licenses by endorsement. Submit your applications well ahead of your anticipated start date.

2. **Secure Two Collaborating Physicians:** If you intend to prescribe medications, remember that Pennsylvania specifically requires a prescriptive authority collaborative agreement with at least **two physicians**. This is unique to PA.

3. **Complete Mandatory State Training:** To renew your license, you must complete **2 hours** of approved child abuse recognition and reporting training every two years.

4. **Monitor Your Pharmacology CEs:** If you hold prescriptive authority, **2 of your 30 required biennial CE hours** must be specifically dedicated to pain management, identification of addiction, or practices of prescribing/dispensing opioids.

5. **Consider Rural PA:** Central and northern Pennsylvania have significant HPSA designations, offering better loan repayment eligibility. Geisinger's rural presence makes it a natural employer for PMHNPs seeking both competitive pay and loan forgiveness.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Pennsylvania a full practice authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: Currently, Pennsylvania is a reduced practice state. You must maintain a written collaborative agreement with a physician to practice, and at least two physicians to prescribe. Check with the Board of Nursing regarding the current implementation status of Act 112 for experienced NPs.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Pennsylvania prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes. However, Schedule II prescriptions are strictly limited to a 30-day supply, and Schedule III-V medications are limited to a 90-day supply.

**Q: Is Pennsylvania part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Yes. Pennsylvania has enacted the NLC, allowing RNs with a multistate license to practice here. However, you still need a PA-specific CRNP license for advanced practice.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my PA PMHNP license?**
A: You must complete 30 contact hours every two years. This must include 2 hours of child abuse recognition and reporting training. Additionally, prescribers must complete 2 hours related to pain management or opioid addiction.
