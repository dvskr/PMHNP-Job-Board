Iowa is an exceptional state for Psychiatric-Mental Health Nurse Practitioners (PMHNPs) seeking high compensation and clinical autonomy. Ranking **4th in the nation** for NP salary when adjusted for cost of living, Iowa provides an incredibly efficient economic environment for psychiatric specialists.

Despite these excellent conditions, the state currently only meets **19.52%** of its mental healthcare needs and requires **83 additional practitioners** to eliminate its designated shortage areas. To help meet this critical demand, Iowa offers Full Practice Authority (FPA) to Advanced Registered Nurse Practitioners (ARNPs). Here is your complete, step-by-step guide to obtaining your PMHNP license in the Hawkeye State for 2026.

## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

Iowa uses a **3-year licensing cycle**. Out-of-state endorsement takes approximately **2-8 weeks**.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited master's, post-master's, or doctoral program with a psychiatric-mental health specialty focus.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

Maintain active national certification to practice as an ARNP in Iowa.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your IA RN License

Iowa is a **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) state**. If your primary state of residence is another NLC state, your multistate RN license is valid here.

### Step 4: Apply for Your IA ARNP License

Submit your Advanced Registered Nurse Practitioner application to the Iowa Board of Nursing with verification of national certification.

### Step 5: Register with the Iowa Board of Pharmacy

To prescribe controlled substances, Iowa ARNPs must register specifically with the **state Board of Pharmacy**.

> **Important:** Your DEA registration alone is not enough — Board of Pharmacy registration is a separate requirement.

### Step 6: Register with the DEA

Apply for your federal DEA registration using your Iowa practice address.

- **Fees:** ~$888 for 3 years

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## IA Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Iowa is a **Full Practice Authority** state.

**No collaborative agreement needed:**
ARNPs can practice to the full extent of their license, education, and experience without a collaborative agreement or physician oversight. You can independently:
- Assess health status
- Order diagnostic procedures
- Formulate differential diagnoses
- Develop treatment plans

**Day-to-day impact:**
Iowa legally defines ARNPs as **primary care providers**. Hospitals **cannot** deny clinical privileges to ARNPs solely based on license type.

> **Note on patient forms:** Iowa ARNPs **cannot** sign DNR orders but **can** sign POST (Physician Orders for Scope of Treatment) forms.

## Prescriptive Authority in IA

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
An ARNP licensed by the board may independently prescribe, administer, or dispense prescription drugs or devices.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Iowa allows full independent prescribing of **Schedule II through V** controlled substances.

**Board of Pharmacy requirement:**
Legend drug authority is included in your licensure, but you must register with the **Iowa Board of Pharmacy** to prescribe controlled substances.

## Telehealth Rules in IA

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Iowa features extensive reimbursement for both live video telehealth and Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM).

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Iowa is an NLC member. However, the **APRN Compact is not enacted** — out-of-state PMHNPs must hold a valid **Iowa ARNP license** to treat patients in IA via telehealth.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to IA

1. **Adapt to the 3-Year Cycle:** Unlike most states, Iowa operates on a **3-year renewal cycle** with **36 CE hours** required per period.

2. **Complete Mandatory Abuse Training:** If you examine, attend, counsel, or treat dependent adults or children, Iowa requires **2 CE hours** related to child and dependent adult abuse.

3. **Log Your Opioid CEs:** If you prescribe opioids during your renewal cycle, complete **2 hours** on CDC guidelines for prescribing opioids for chronic pain.

4. **Register with the Board of Pharmacy:** Don't assume your DEA registration covers controlled substance prescribing — you must separately register with the **Iowa Board of Pharmacy**.

5. **Consider Rural Iowa:** Over 80% of Iowa's counties have mental health provider shortages, with maximum NHSC eligibility and excellent quality of life.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Iowa a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: Yes. Iowa is a Full Practice Authority state. ARNPs can independently assess, diagnose, treat, and prescribe without any physician oversight or collaborative agreement.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Iowa prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes, independently. You must hold an active DEA registration and be registered with the Iowa Board of Pharmacy.

**Q: Is Iowa part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Yes. Iowa is a full NLC member. However, the APRN Compact is not enacted — an Iowa-specific ARNP license is required.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my IA PMHNP license?**
A: Complete 36 CE hours every three years and maintain national certification. Additional requirements include mandatory reporter training (2 hrs) and opioid prescribing guidelines (2 hrs if you prescribe opioids).
