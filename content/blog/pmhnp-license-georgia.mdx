## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

The path to becoming a licensed PMHNP in Georgia requires coordinating with both the Board of Nursing and a delegating physician.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited post-basic educational program for Nurse Practitioners that includes advanced pharmacology in its curriculum or as a separate course.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

You must maintain current national advanced practice specialty certification.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your Georgia RN License

Georgia is a **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) state**. If your primary state of residence is another NLC state, your multistate RN license covers you in Georgia. If not, apply for a single-state Georgia RN license.

### Step 4: Apply for Your Georgia APRN License

Submit your APRN application to the Georgia Board of Nursing.

| Item | Cost |
|:---|:---|
| Endorsement Fee | $75 |
| Background Check | Via GOALS portal (varies) |

- **Documents:** Transcripts, national certification, fingerprints through the **GOALS portal**
- **Processing time:** 3-6 weeks
- **Note:** The Board does **not** typically issue temporary permits

### Step 5: Establish a Protocol Agreement with a Physician

To practice and prescribe legally, you must establish a **written nurse protocol agreement** with a delegating physician, which must be submitted to the Board.

- The agreement specifies the exact medical acts delegated to you
- Must include a provision for **immediate consultation** with your delegating physician

### Step 6: Register with the DEA

Once your protocol agreement is approved, register with the DEA to prescribe controlled substances.

- **Fees:** ~$888 for 3 years
- **Requirement:** Use your Georgia practice address

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## Georgia Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Georgia strictly enforces a **Restricted Practice** model.

**Protocol agreement requirements:**
You cannot practice independently. Georgia law mandates that PMHNPs work under a **written nurse protocol agreement** with a delegating physician. This document specifies the exact medical acts delegated to you — ordering drugs, medical devices, medical treatments, and diagnostic studies. The agreement must also include a provision for immediate consultation with your delegating physician.

**Pending FPA legislation:**
Advocacy efforts for expanded NP practice authority are ongoing, but no significant legislative changes toward Full Practice Authority have occurred in recent years.

> **Day-to-day impact:** Because of these strict supervision requirements, PMHNPs in Georgia frequently find the most stable and seamless roles within large academic medical centers or major health systems (like Emory Healthcare or Wellstar), where standardized protocols and physician access are already heavily integrated into the workflow.

## Prescriptive Authority in Georgia

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
Under your written protocol agreement, your delegating physician can authorize you to order dangerous drugs, medical treatments, and controlled substances.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Historically, Georgia prohibited NPs from prescribing Schedule II drugs. However, current regulations allow APRNs to prescribe **Schedules III-V** controlled substances, as well as **specific Schedule II medications** (such as hydrocodone, oxycodone, or compounds thereof), provided the authority is explicitly outlined in the submitted written protocol with the supervising physician.

**Formulary restrictions:**
Your prescribing limits are directly tied to what your delegating physician agrees to in your collaborative protocol.

## Telehealth Rules in Georgia

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Georgia maintains an active statewide telehealth network, and live video (synchronous) telehealth services are widely reimbursed by Medicaid and private payers.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
While Georgia is an NLC state for RNs, it is **not** part of the APRN Compact. Out-of-state PMHNPs wishing to provide telehealth services to patients physically located in Georgia must hold an active **Georgia APRN license**.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities allowing providers to prescribe controlled substances via telemedicine have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to Georgia

1. **Target Large Health Systems:** Navigating restricted practice is easier at major employers like Emory or Piedmont, which already have streamlined protocol agreements and accessible supervising physicians on staff.

2. **Complete Background Checks Early:** Georgia requires fingerprinting through the specific **GOALS portal**. Delays here will stall your endorsement, so initiate this step immediately upon applying.

3. **Join UAPRN of Georgia:** Get involved with the **United Advanced Practice Registered Nurses of Georgia (UAPRN)**. They are the primary organization advocating for expanded NP practice authority and reduced restrictions in the state legislature.

4. **Leverage Telehealth:** Georgia's active statewide telehealth network and favorable reimbursement parity make remote and hybrid psychiatric roles highly viable, even in restricted settings.

5. **No State Income Tax on Retirement:** While Georgia does have state income tax, it offers favorable deductions — and the cost of living is significantly lower than CA, NY, or FL coastal metros. Your $171K goes further here.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Are PMHNPs allowed to prescribe medication in Georgia?**
A: Yes. PMHNPs can prescribe medications, including Schedule III-V and certain Schedule II controlled substances (like hydrocodone and oxycodone), provided they are working under a formal, written nurse protocol agreement with a delegating physician.

**Q: Is Georgia a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: No. Georgia is a Restricted Practice state. You must have a career-long supervisory agreement or protocol with a physician to provide patient care and prescribe medications.

**Q: How many CE hours do I need to renew my Georgia PMHNP license?**
A: You are required to complete 30 contact hours of continuing education every two years. Maintaining your active national certification typically satisfies this requirement.

**Q: Can I practice telehealth in Georgia if I live in another state?**
A: Yes, but because Georgia has not joined the APRN Compact, you must obtain a single-state Georgia APRN license to treat patients physically located within Georgia's borders.
