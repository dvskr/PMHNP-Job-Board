## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

Hawaii is **not** an NLC state. Endorsement takes **7-9 weeks** — plan accordingly.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited advanced practice program. Must document at least **30 contact hours** of advanced pharmacology education.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

Maintain active national certification.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your HI RN License

Hawaii is **not** an NLC state (HB 897 pending). You must apply for a **single-state Hawaii RN license** by endorsement.

### Step 4: Apply for Your HI APRN License

Submit your APRN application to the Hawaii Board of Nursing via the Department of Commerce and Consumer Affairs.

### Step 5: Apply for Prescriptive Authority

File a **separate application** for prescriptive authority with DCCA. Requirements:
- Proof of 30 advanced pharmacology hours
- Established **collegial working relationship agreement** with a physician

### Step 6: Register with the DEA

Apply for your federal DEA registration using your Hawaii practice address.

- **Fees:** ~$888 for 3 years

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## HI Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Hawaii is a **Full Practice Authority** state.

APRNs are **legally recognized as primary care providers** — health insurance companies can formally designate a participating NP as a PCP. You can independently:
- Evaluate patients and diagnose conditions
- Manage acute, chronic, and end-of-life treatments
- Serve as medical staff in hospitals and licensed facilities

**Day-to-day impact:**
You can independently sign:
- ✅ DNR orders
- ✅ POLST forms
- ✅ Medical certifications

## Prescriptive Authority in HI

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
PMHNPs can prescribe medications, therapeutic measures, OTC drugs, legend drugs, and medical devices within their specialty.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Full prescribing of **Schedule II through V** controlled substances.

**Collegial working relationship:**
Hawaii requires a **collegial working relationship agreement** with a physician, which must be filed with your prescriptive authority application.

## Telehealth Rules in HI

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — telehealth is essential for inter-island care. Hawaii offers broad coverage and is supported by the Pacific Basin Telehealth Resource Center.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Hawaii is **not** a compact state. Out-of-state PMHNPs must hold an active **single-state Hawaii APRN license**.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to HI

1. **Anticipate Licensing Delays:** Hawaii endorsement takes **7-9 weeks** — apply well before your target start date.

2. **Establish Your Collegial Agreement:** Secure a physician willing to sign your **collegial working relationship agreement** before applying for prescriptive authority.

3. **Prepare Your Pharmacology Documentation:** Have transcripts ready verifying **30 contact hours** of advanced pharmacology education.

4. **Know Your CE Breakdown:** Biennial renewal requires 30 CE hours: **22 in your specialty** and **8-10 in pharmacology**.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Hawaii a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: Yes. APRNs are legally recognized as primary care providers with full clinical independence.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Hawaii prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes, with active prescriptive authority and DEA registration. A collegial working relationship agreement with a physician is required.

**Q: Is Hawaii part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Not yet. Legislation (HB 897) is pending. Currently, a single-state Hawaii license is required.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my HI PMHNP license?**
A: Complete 30 CE hours every two years — 22 in your specialty area and 8-10 in pharmacology.
