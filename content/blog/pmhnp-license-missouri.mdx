## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

Missouri's endorsement process is notoriously slow — plan for **8-12 weeks** processing time.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited advanced practice program. If your specialty lacks a clinical certification exam, Missouri requires **3 graduate credit hours of pharmacology**.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

You must maintain active national certification to practice and renew.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your MO RN License

Missouri is a **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) state**. If your primary state of residence is another NLC state, your multistate RN license covers you here.

### Step 4: Apply for MO APRN License

Submit your APRN application to the Missouri Board of Nursing.

- **Processing time:** 8-12 weeks (plan ahead!)

### Step 5: Establish a Collaborative Agreement with a Physician

Establish a **career-long written collaborative practice arrangement** with a supervising physician before providing patient care.

- Must outline geographic practice areas for both you and your collaborating physician

### Step 6: Register for BNDD and DEA

To prescribe controlled substances, you need **three** credentials:

| Credential | Details |
|:---|:---|
| **Certificate of Controlled Substance Prescriptive Authority** | From the Board — requires 1,000 hrs general practice + 300 hrs precepted prescribing |
| **Missouri BNDD Registration** | State Bureau of Narcotics and Dangerous Drugs |
| **Federal DEA Registration** | ~$888 for 3 years |

> **Important:** You cannot apply for controlled substance authority until you complete **1,000 hours of general practice** and **300 hours of precepted prescribing experience**.

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## MO Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Missouri strictly enforces a **Restricted Practice** model.

**Geographic proximity rules:**
Missouri law requires your collaborative practice agreement to explicitly outline the geographic practice areas for both you and your collaborating physician.

**NPs not recognized as primary care providers:**
By legal definition, NPs in Missouri are **not** recognized as primary care providers.

**Pending FPA legislation:**
Bills are being evaluated that would allow qualifying NPs with **2,000 documented hours** of supervision to end their collaborative agreement entirely, and legislation to address geographic proximity restrictions.

> **Day-to-day impact:** You cannot practice independently, and you do **not** have the authority to sign POLST forms.

## Prescriptive Authority in MO

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
Under your collaborative agreement, your physician can delegate authority to prescribe, dispense, and administer medications and non-scheduled legend drugs.

**Schedule II-V rules with strict guardrails:**

| Schedule | Limitation |
|:---|:---|
| **Schedule II** | **Hydrocodone combination products ONLY** — 5-day supply max |
| **Schedule III** | 5-day supply max |
| **Schedule IV-V** | Standard prescribing under collaborative agreement |

> **Critical for PMHNP practice:** If your practice typically relies on Schedule II stimulants (e.g., ADHD medications like methylphenidate or amphetamine salts), Missouri's restrictions will significantly impact your prescribing workflows. Plan accordingly.

**Requirements:**
Must hold state BNDD registration + federal DEA license + Certificate of Controlled Substance Prescriptive Authority.

## Telehealth Rules in MO

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Missouri offers broad store-and-forward capabilities and parity for live video telehealth.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Missouri is an NLC member. However, the **APRN Compact is not active** — out-of-state PMHNPs must secure a **Missouri APRN license** and a local collaborative physician agreement.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities allowing providers to prescribe controlled substances via telemedicine have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to MO

1. **Prepare for Licensing Delays:** Missouri's Board of Nursing takes **8-12 weeks** for endorsement. Submit well in advance.

2. **Track Your Prescribing Hours:** You cannot apply for controlled substance authority until you log **1,000 hours of general practice** and **300 hours of precepted prescribing experience**.

3. **Obtain Your BNDD:** Missouri requires a state **Bureau of Narcotics and Dangerous Drugs** registration in addition to your federal DEA to prescribe controlled substances.

4. **Know Your Schedule II Limits:** Missouri restricts Schedule II prescribing to **hydrocodone combination products only** with a 5-day supply max. If your practice relies on stimulants (ADHD meds), plan your workflows carefully.

5. **Consider the Ozarks:** Rural southern Missouri and the Bootheel have among the most severe provider shortages, offering maximum loan repayment eligibility.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Missouri a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: No. Missouri is a restricted practice state. PMHNPs must maintain a career-long collaborative practice arrangement with a supervising physician.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Missouri prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes, but with severe restrictions. Only hydrocodone combination products are allowed, limited to a 5-day supply maximum. You must hold a BNDD registration, DEA license, and Certificate of Controlled Substance Prescriptive Authority.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my MO PMHNP license?**
A: Missouri does not require specific CE contact hours. APRNs must simply maintain active national certification.

**Q: Do I need a separate registration to prescribe controlled substances in Missouri?**
A: Yes. You need three credentials: a Certificate of Controlled Substance Prescriptive Authority from the Board, a state BNDD registration, and a federal DEA registration.
