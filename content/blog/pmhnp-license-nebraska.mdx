## Step-by-Step Licensure Guide

NLC multistate RN license is valid here. Single-state endorsement takes **3-10 weeks**.

### Step 1: Complete MSN or DNP with PMHNP Focus

Graduate from an accredited advanced practice program. Education must include at least **30 hours of pharmacotherapeutics**.

### Step 2: Pass the ANCC PMHNP-BC Exam

Maintain active national certification.

| Membership Status | Exam Fee |
|:---|:---|
| Non-member | $395 |
| ANA member | $295 |
| AANP member | $340 |

Learn more about the [PMHNP-BC credential](/blog/what-does-pmhnp-bc-mean)

### Step 3: Apply for Your NE RN License

Nebraska is a **Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC) state**. If your primary state of residence is another NLC state, your multistate RN license covers you here.

### Step 4: Apply for Your NE APRN License

Submit your APRN application to the Board of Advanced Practice Registered Nurses.

### Step 5: Complete Transition-to-Practice

New APRNs must complete **2,000 hours of clinical practice** under a supervising provider before practicing fully independently.

### Step 6: Register with the DEA

Prescriptive authority is included with licensure. Register with the federal DEA for controlled substances.

- **Fees:** ~$888 for 3 years

### Step 7: Apply for an NPI Number

Obtain your National Provider Identifier (NPI) via the [CMS NPPES system](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov/) (Free).

## NE Practice Authority Details

**Current status:** Nebraska is a **Full Practice Authority** state with a structured transition.

### 2,000-Hour Transition

| Phase | Details |
|:---|:---|
| **During transition** | Practice under supervising provider; Schedule II limited to 72-hour supply |
| **After 2,000 hours** | Full independent practice — no supervision required |

> **Day-to-day impact:** Nebraska does **not** legally define NPs as primary care providers. NPs cannot sign POLST forms, but **can** pronounce death and sign death certificates.

## Prescriptive Authority in NE

**What can PMHNPs prescribe?**
PMHNPs can evaluate, diagnose, prescribe therapeutic measures and medications, and dispense manufacturer samples.

**Schedule II-V rules:**
Full prescribing of **Schedule II through V** controlled substances.

**Transition period limitation:**

| Period | Schedule II Rule |
|:---|:---|
| **First 2,000 hours** | Maximum **72-hour supply** per prescription |
| **After 2,000 hours** | Full independent prescribing — no supply limits |

## Telehealth Rules in NE

**Can PMHNPs do telehealth?**
Yes — Nebraska provides Medicaid reimbursement for live video telehealth and RPM.

**Out-of-state requirements:**
Nebraska is an NLC member. The **APRN Compact is not enacted** — out-of-state PMHNPs must hold an active **Nebraska APRN license**.

**Controlled substance teleprescribing:**
Federal DEA and HHS flexibilities have been extended through **December 31, 2027**.

## Tips for PMHNPs Moving to NE

1. **Plan for Endorsement Timeframes:** Nebraska Board processing can take **3-10 weeks** — begin paperwork well before your start date.

2. **Track Your 72-Hour Rule:** During your first 2,000 hours, do **not** exceed the 72-hour supply limit for Schedule II prescriptions.

3. **Complete Opioid CEs:** If you hold DEA registration, complete **3 hours** of CE on prescribing opiates every renewal cycle.

4. **Audit Your Transcripts:** Ensure your graduate transcripts show at least **30 hours of pharmacotherapeutics** — a state requirement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Nebraska a Full Practice Authority state for PMHNPs?**
A: Yes, with a 2,000-hour transition period. After completing supervised practice, APRNs practice completely independently.

**Q: Can PMHNPs in Nebraska prescribe Schedule II controlled substances?**
A: Yes. During the 2,000-hour transition, Schedule II is limited to a 72-hour supply. After transition, full independent prescribing.

**Q: Is Nebraska part of the Nurse Licensure Compact (NLC)?**
A: Yes. The APRN Compact is not enacted — a Nebraska-specific APRN license is required.

**Q: What are the CE requirements to renew my NE PMHNP license?**
A: Complete 20 CE hours every two years, maintain national certification, document 500 practice hours, and complete 3 opioid CE hours if prescribing controlled substances.
